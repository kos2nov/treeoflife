Thai Massage

Traditional Thai Massage is performed on a large floor mat and the recipient remains fully clothed. Each session begins by working the muscles of your feet, and then continues working with compression techniques and manipulation of acupressure points. Muscles of the legs and hips are addressed before the back and shoulders. ﻿The stretches, lengthen and loosen muscles and are designed to balance and realign the body over a period of time. When combined, these techniques flow in a comforting rhythm that lets your body fall into a deep state of relaxation. Thai massage is a unique, ancient, and time-tested form of bodywork.

Thai Herbal Massage
Herbal healing has deep roots in Thai culture. Herbal heat compress is the best-known traditional therapy. This dates back to ancient times when it was the secret healing technique of Buddhist monks, local herbal healers and midwives.
The treatment begins with a deep-pressure Thai massage, then a unique poultice of steamed Thai herbal ingredients applied to help sooth and relax muscle tension and other aches and pains.
Aromatic camphor in the compress eases respiration. Traditional Thai medicine uses herbal heat compression for the treatment of sprains, muscle pain, bruises and swelling. Anti-inflammatory properties of the Thai herbs soothe and calm the muscle into a more relaxed state.

﻿Nerve Touch
Nerve Touch also known as "Jap Sen" massage, meaning to grasp the sen. This is a deeper approach with focus on therapy for joints, nerves and relieving painful conditions. It applies stronger pressure using knees, elbows, feet, heels and thumbs. Master Lek Chaiya has developed this unique and very therapeutic style of Thai massage. There is a rolling over or "gentle twanging" of the muscles and nerves for deep release of tension and therapeutic benefit for conditions such as sciatica, back pain, carpal tunnel syndrome, numbness, paralysis, frozen shoulder and many other conditions.

Thai Foot Massage is an ancient Chinese massage and has been in use for more than 3,000 years. The theory is that the sensory nerves of the body’s inner organs are spread throughout the body and are connected to the most superficial layer of the body, such as the skin. All sensory nerves are rooted primarily at the bottom of the foot. Massaging and pressing specific points on the soles of the feet associated with particular organs produces a therapeutic effect in corresponding parts of the body, helps stimulate activities of the inner organs and improves blood and lymph circulation.
Thai Foot Reflexology combined with Traditional Thai massage is good for ensuring good health and well being, relieves pain, muscle tension & stiffness.

Therapeutic Massage

Therapeutic massage does more than just relax and eliminate stress from the body. It is also an alternative used to relieve pain. People are often surprised to see how effective massage therapy can be.

Chronic pain in the muscle is typically an indication of muscle tightness. Trauma, injury, muscle repetition, and even stress can develop into a chronic condition if left untreated. When muscle tighten, the fibers within the muscle decreases in length resulting in a shortened muscle. This shortened muscle, along with a connective tissue called fascia, can begin to adhere to surrounding structures in the body, decreasing range of motion in the joints and placing pressure on nerves. Tight muscles can also lead to reduced circulation, which allows waste to accumulate. Waste irritates the nerves, therefore causing pain.

Therapeutic massage relieve the pain by increasing circulation, softening and lengthening tight muscle and fascia, and breaking down any scar tissue or adhesions that have formed between tissue layers.

How can muscle pain be prevented?

Prevention is the key to keeping our bodies in healthy condition. Our bodies need regular tune-ups, just like our cars, to keep them performing efficiently and without problems. Getting a massage regularly enables the body to release unwanted stress from the muscle and helps you feel your best.
